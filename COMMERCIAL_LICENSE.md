# GenSec — Commercial Licensing

## Why a Commercial License?

GenSec is distributed under the **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0
(AGPL-3.0)**.  This means you can freely use, modify, and redistribute GenSec
— as long as you make your modifications and any software that interacts with
GenSec over a network available under the same AGPL-3.0 terms.

For organizations that **cannot or do not wish** to comply with the AGPL-3.0
obligations, a **commercial license** is available.

---

## When Do You Need a Commercial License?

You need a commercial license if **any** of the following apply:

1. **Proprietary integration** — You embed GenSec (or parts of it) into
   proprietary software and do not wish to release your source code under
   AGPL-3.0.

2. **SaaS / hosted services** — You offer GenSec's functionality as part of a
   web service, cloud platform, or API without releasing the complete source
   code of the service.

3. **Redistribution** — You distribute GenSec (modified or unmodified) as part
   of a commercial product, SDK, or toolkit.

---

## When You Do NOT Need a Commercial License

- **Internal engineering calculations** — Using GenSec internally for your own
  structural analysis projects, regardless of company size, is permitted under
  the AGPL-3.0 as long as you do not redistribute the software.

- **Academic and educational use** — Universities, research labs, and students
  may use GenSec freely under the AGPL-3.0.

- **Open-source projects** — If your project is also AGPL-3.0-compatible, no
  commercial license is required.

---

## Contribution-Based Waivers

Organizations that make **significant, sustained contributions** to GenSec may
be eligible for a fee waiver or discount on the commercial license.
Qualifying contributions include:

- Major feature implementations merged into the main repository
- Comprehensive test suites or validation campaigns
- Ongoing maintenance of modules or subsystems

Eligibility is assessed on a case-by-case basis.  Contact the Author to
discuss.

## Pricing

Commercial licenses are negotiated individually based on:

- Scope of use (single product vs. enterprise-wide)
- Redistribution volume
- Duration (annual or perpetual)
